ApplicantPro Dashboard Improvements
2018 — Interaction
ApplicantPro helps companies "hire better". One of the ways they do this is through their in-house job ad writers.

Writers use an internal tool to write, edit and approve writing requests. For this project, I quickly improved the dashboard and edited the internal tool. Many of the changes focused on reorganizing information and adding functionality improvements.

Original internal job board dashboard.

The first development of this tool did not have a designer involved. Eventually the writers requested a redesign to make the dashboard user friendly and add more functionality.

After working with the product manager and speaking with the job ad writing team, I compiled a list of their goals for the redesign:
  • Improve usability— organize the information in a better way.
  • Add the ability to assign writers to specific job ads.
  • Change the design to meet ApplicantPro’s updated branding guidelines (mainly typeface changes).

Internal dashboard after redesign.

The UI improvements were simple, but effective. Some of the changes included adding color to create contrast, giving the type styles defined hierarchy to help organize the data, and arranging sections in different orientations. The ability to assign specific writers in a dropdown was also added.

Dropdown and paginating styles were addressed but couldn't be updated without changing global styles—so that was shelved.

For reviews with the project manager and job writers, I provided a version of the design with detailed reasoning for the design changes.

After implementation, the job writers reported that the new layout allowed them to work quicker and made training new hires much easier.
