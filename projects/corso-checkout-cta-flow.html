Corso Checkout CTA Flow
2022 — Interaction, Motion, Brand
Corso is an exciting startup that helps e-commerce companies and individuals offset shipping carbon while securing their packages. The company has the unique challenge of educating consumers on both sustainability and secure shipping.
‍
Corso asked me to take a look at the flow of one of their CTA’s that is included on their partners checkout. There were two parts of this project, the main CTA and an educational modal accessed through the CTA.
‍
The main goal was to quickly improve the overall user experience while allowing the brand voice to change if needed.

Original CTA

Corso adds a simple CTA at the end of their e-commerce partner’s checkout.
‍
This CTA is meant to be subtle and uses the e-commerce partner’s type style. One of the goals of the CTA is to not interrupt the shopper’s check out experience. If the secondary copy is clicked, a modal will pop up to provide further education on Corso's service.
‍
Things I noticed about the CTA that could be improved:
  • The copy should better describe Corso’s service.
  • The informational "i" in the top messaging is too small. It looked like a dot on the screen.
  • Other than the mouse-hover interaction, there was no indication that clicking on the secondary copy would open a modal, making it so curious shoppers miss the opportunity to open the modal.

Proposed redesign

My solutions were simple:
  • Revise the bottom copy to be more descriptive of the service.
  • Remove the "i" in the top messaging—it wasn't adding anything to the design.
  • Shoppers need to know there is more information about the service. Underlining the link that opens the education modal helps to indicate there is an interaction waiting if clicked.

Original service education modal.

After meeting with Corso, we considered the following with the redesign:
  • Currently the modal feels plain, how can we incorporate more of Corso's brand voice?
  • If a partner wants to use their branding, could we create a more modular approach that can allow different brands to be placed in the modal?
  • There are two separate stories being told in the service. One is package security and the other is environmental responsibility. Is there a subtle way that we can highlight both without adding a ton of copy?

Figma file showing different iterations of modal.

Final modal with animated GIF header.

Subsequent design updates include:
  • Total update to better reflect Corso's brand. Following brand guidelines, I incorporated their brand typefaces (Migra and Centra No1) and created imagery that reflects their brand voice.
  • The addition of a large header that is interchangeable with animated assets or static assets helps to tell their story of sustainability.
  • Adding spacing and a stronger type hierarchy elevates the design, making it feel digestible and clean.
  • Corso wants to give their larger partners the opportunity to customize this modal. The new design makes it easier for their partner’s brands to show through in the modal.
